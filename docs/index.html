My Story

Let's take it back to the beginning

  • 1997-2012

    It takes a village

    In the Spring of 1997 I became the first-born of my two wonderful parents, immigrants from the island nation of Dominica. Being born and raised in South Miami really helped shaped me into the person I am today. However, it was the carpentry work of my father that inspired me to become a creator.

    Photo by Ryan Parker on Unsplash

  • 2012-2015

    High School Musical Robotical

    I was a student in the Robotics Academy at TERRA Environmental Research Institute, but after 2 years, I found myself pursuing an opportunity more in line with my goals at the School for Advanced Studies (SAS). To keep my creative spirit alive, I became one of the founding members of the FIRST Robotics team (Team 5329) at SAS. My experiences with the team really helped inform my decisions around how I wanted to navigate higher education.

  • 2015-2020

    Ever heard of it?

    In 2015 I enrolled in Cornell University's College of Engineering, deciding to study Computer Science in my second year. I've been a TA for Artificial Intelligence as well as both of the Introductory and the Intermediate levels of our Web Development courses. Most recently, I was a TA for the Data-Driven Web Applications course. I was the Marketing Director for the BigRed//Hacks Hackathon Organizing team. I am also a member of our Underrepresented Minorities in Computing organization and our Association of Computer Science Undergrads.

  • 2017-2019

    INTRN SZN

    Since Sophomore year, I've spent every summer in the Bay Area doing Software Engineering Internship work. In 2017 I was a Mobile Engineering Intern for WalmartLabs where I built a mobile app in Electrode Native (an expansion of the React Native framework). In 2018 I worked on Walmart's International team, doing backend work on their Google Assistant app for the grocery chain ASDA. In 2019, I performed data visualization and analysis in Python for one of Google's hardware teams.

  • 2020

    Hey Siri, play One More Time

    In my final semester of undergrad, I elected to begin an early admit Masters of Engineering (M.Eng) degree. This has given me the ability to complete my M.Eng in one extra semester this Fall. I am crafting my experience in this degree to focus on the Software Engineering topics I found most interesting in undergrad; Data-Driven Software Applications and the Human-Centered Design process.

Cornell Hyperloop

Electrical Team Lead

A team of students building a high speed Pod to compete in the Hyperloop Pod Competition run by SpaceX. In my 3 years on the team I served as a team member, the Electrical Hardware Subteam Lead, and finally the Electrical Team Lead. I was in charge of overseeing the construction of our team's electrical subsystem which involved work with embedded systems, various sensors, and Arduinos as well as the software that went into working with these hardware components.

  • January 2017 - May 2019
  • Visit Team Page

OVegas

Full-Stack Engineer

OVegas is an implementation of the classic casino game Texas Hold'em in the Functional Programming language OCaml. My back-end work involved creating some of the structures that the game required to operate. My front-end work focused on the design and implementation of the Terminal interface (prompts, ASCII art, etc.) that players used to interact with the game.

  • November 2017 - December 2017
  • GitHub Repo

Career Fair Queue

iOS Front-End Engineer

A system for improving the Career Fair experience for both students and recruiters. The iOS app for the system allowed students to register for Career Fairs, prioritize which companies they wanted to speak to, upload resumes, and more. I created some of the mockups for the app and worked on implementing the app's UI.

  • January 2019 - May 2019
  • Client: Thomas Hull IV

Uncommon App

Front-End Engineer

An Information Retrieval system that allows users to submit queries with some of their performance measures from high school (GPA, test scores, etc.) and things they're looking for in a university. These queries are then used to provide a ranked list of schools that could be a good fit for the student.
